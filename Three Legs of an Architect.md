The stool metaphor is apt from Gregor Hohpe. 

> Skills -> Impact -> Leadership

>
**Hohpe’s View on Skill**: Skill is the easiest to acquire (through courses/books) but it has a "glass ceiling." If you only have skill, you are just an expert who does what they are told.

>
**Hohpe’s View on Impact**: Skill without impact is just a hobby. To have impact, you must understand the "why" behind the business decisions and translate your technical work into results (money saved, speed increased, risks reduced).

>
**Hohpe’s View on Leadership**: Leadership is how you scale your impact. Instead of solving one problem, you teach 100 people how to solve it. This is what truly marks a "High Confidence Hire."


**Warning:** Hohpe warns that if you miss one leg, your career "stool" falls over:

**Skill + Impact - Leadership**: You are a *"Solution Architect"*—great at your job, but stuck in a single project. You'll hit a ceiling because you aren't growing the organization.

**Skill + Leadership - Impact**: You are an *"Ivory Tower Architect"*—you talk a lot and teach others, but your advice isn't grounded in real-world results.

**Impact + Leadership - Skill:** You are a *"PowerPoint Architect"*—you have influence, but your technical decisions are shallow or impractical.
